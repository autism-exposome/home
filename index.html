Welcome to Autism Exposome Project


Autism Exposome Project aims to investigate what contributed a significant increase to the autism prevalence of the U.S., focusing on discovery of internal and external environmental exposures that are detectable in the blood from the patients with autism and their mothers, and the role these exposures play in gene function in autism.


The collaborative activities of the Autism Exposome Project have been divided amongst four leading organizations: Boston children's Hospital, Harvard medical school, University of Emory and University of Tufts. Read more


This project is funded by the National Institutes of Health National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences (NIEHS).
